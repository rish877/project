About the Air Quality Index (AQI)

Components of the WQI

  • 0-50 (Good): Air quality in this range is considered good, with little or no risk to health. Outdoor activities are generally safe.
  • 51-100 (Moderate): Air quality is acceptable; however, sensitive individuals may have slight concerns. Most people can be outdoors safely.
  • 101-150 (Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups): Air quality is unhealthy for sensitive groups, such as children, the elderly, or those with respiratory conditions. Limit outdoor exertion.
  • 151-200 (Unhealthy): Air quality is considered unhealthy for everyone. Outdoor activities should be avoided, especially for sensitive individuals.
  • 201-300 (Very Unhealthy): Air quality in this range is very unhealthy for everyone and may trigger serious health effects. Stay indoors, and use air filtration if necessary.
  • 301-500 (Hazardous): The air quality is hazardous, posing a health risk for everyone. Emergency measures are needed to protect public health. Stay indoors, and avoid outdoor activities.
